The Universe

Soundverse

A fictional universe held together by music. The characters exist between tracks — their histories written in frequencies, their futures deferred into the next song.

Soundverse began as a question: what if a music project had a mythology instead of a biography? What if the songs weren't windows into a life but coordinates in a space that doesn't obey the same rules as the one you're standing in right now?

The universe is built outward from Arq — a character defined less by what they are and more by the resonance they leave in every place they pass through. The music is the universe's primary document. Everything else is annotation.

Lore is added as the music is made. If a song ends and you don't know quite where you are, that's intentional. Ambiguity is load-bearing here.

The World

The Frequency

What Soundverse Is

Not a place. Not a timeline. A frequency — a band of possibility that certain consciousnesses can tune into. Arq was either born there or drifted in. The distinction matters less than you'd think.

The Arcs

Origin Arc

The earliest phase of the universe — when Arq's signal was still finding its shape. The music from this period is rawer, more uncertain. Red Ink is its first document. More pending.

The Rules

What Holds It Together

The universe has rules but doesn't explain them. You're expected to infer. The music is always primary — if a song contradicts a lore page, the song is right. Placeholder lore will be replaced as songs are written.
